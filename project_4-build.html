Games have always been an intricate part of most people’s childhood, and what better game can you think of than when you played rock, paper, scissors as a child? Then let's play a game where rock is "penny", paper is "cloth", and scissors is "morgan". While the names aren’t the best, it is still a great game that we hope you will enjoy.

According to Wikipedia, the game "Rock", "Paper", and "Scissors" was first mentioned in the book "Wuzazu" written in 1600 (Ming-dynasty). The writer, Xie Zhaozhi described that it originated as early as 206 B.C. during the Han-dynasty. It was originally named "mushi-ken" from China, and it was imported into Japanese as "sansukumi-ken". Both versions used finger representations of animals, while modern day society has diverted away from these representations. In our case, we have changed the terms to suit our needs, where "penny" stands for a small yet strong force, "cloth" holds power in in flimsiness, and "morgan" is strong and massive. All have the ability to win, sharing the same strength as each other, while all in a different and subtle way. As both Japan and China utilized different animals, each animal that was used, represented their cultures supernatural superstitions for the animal chosen.

As the game grew in popularity, it began to spread across the world. Leaving Asia, a club was created in England in 1842. The Paper Scissors Stone Club, which was later changed in 1918 to "World RPS Club", moved its headquarters to Toronto, Canada in 1925. They even had a championship!

While it is impossible to have an edge over a truly random opponent, newer players are easily exploited for repetition. As most children find out, people alternate in a repetitious manner when the opponent makes a move. The more experienced players will use this to their advantage. This makes the game less random, and easier to win for the advanced player.
