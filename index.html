Welcome

Welcome to Morgan silver dollars! We hope this site will provide all of the information you will need to get started on your own collection. This website was developed for a graduate level web development course at Towson University, in Spring 2017. Although this website was designed for grading purposes, this website does include a vast amount of information that can be found useful.

While many of our pages hold helpful and useful information, we highly recommend you read "Cleaning", "FAQ", "Grading", and "Storage". These pages provide the building blocks to a coin collection. If you are interested in exploring areas in more depth, please move to our "Links" page, where you will be able to see the best websites for the area you want to know more about.

Silver and gold coins were originally the standard form of currency, until the coinage act of 1965. When the bill passed, all coins that were minted with silver were removed from circulation as people started realizing the metal value was dramatically increasing. Half dollars still contained 40% silver, compared to the previous 90%, until 1970 when another bill was passed. Morgan dollars are the most sought after coins, because they are a true ounce of silver. They have a composition of:

Composition:

  1. 90% Silver
  2. 10% Copper

All of the morgen dollars in circulation were minted "Carson City (CC), Nevada", "Denver (D), Colorado", "New Orleans (O), Louisiana", and "San Francisco (S), California". While the mintage rates differed each year, George T. Morgan continued to design and engraved the coins design.
