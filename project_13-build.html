F.A.Q.

Please read through the question and answer section below. If your questions have not been answered, please feel free to contact us using the form at the bottom of the page.

What is a Morgan dollar?

A Morgan dollar was a form of United States currency.

When were Morgan Dollars Minted?

1878-1921

Where were Morgan Dollars Minted?

The mintmark denoting the origin of all Morgan dollars is located on the reverse, bottom center, directly under the wreath.

  • “CC” - Carson City Mint

    These are among the rarest mintmarks, and sought after by collectors. The mintmark to look for from this Mint are “CC”

  • “D” - Denver Mint

    This mint still produces coins to this day; however, they have stopped coining Morgan Dollars 113 years ago. The mintmark to look for from this Mint are “D”

  • “O” - New Orleans

    This is a rarer mintmark, but less rare then Carson City. The mintmark to look for from this Mint are “O”

  • Philadelphia

    This mint still produces coins to this day; however, they have stopped coining Morgan Dollars 113 years ago. This is the only version to not have a mintmark.

  • “S” - San Francisco

    This mint still produces coins to this day; however, they have stopped coining Morgan Dollars 113 years ago. The mintmark to look for from this Mint are “S”

How much silver is in each coin?

Each coin is 90% silver, and 10% copper. With silver prices being around $16 an ounce, silver coins are not as viable as they once were.

Who designed this iconic coin?

“George T. Morgan”

How many coins were created each year at each mint?

Each year, the number produced varied. While one mint may create 1,000,000 coins, another may print 10,000,000.

Do they still print Morgan Dollars?

No, but they do still print $1 coins. These new coins do not have any precious metals contained, but they do have this denomination.

When did they stop producing Morgan Dollars?

1904 and 1921. Why two different years? Because they brought it back for just one year, 17 years after its last date of production!
